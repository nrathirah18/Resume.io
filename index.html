IDP : Palm Oil Soil Monitoring Systems

Implemented during degree studies at UPM
Duration : October 2022 - January 2023

About : Project from Integrated Design Project Course (ECC4947). Currently, oil palm plantations encounter problems of soil erosion, loss of fertilizer and carbon footprint. In addition, most oil palm companies are still using conventional methods in acquiring the soil information. Hence, the purpose of this project is to create a system that will allow for effective data transfer between the sensor at the oil palm plantation and the receiver, which is the operator. Real-time soil monitoring for oil palm plantation is a device that can enhance the task efficiency in oil palm plantation. The device was developed to monitor the soil condition that includes the measurement of soil moisture, soil pH and ambient temperature values with utilisation of Internet of Things (IoT) to send data to the Blynk application. For the purposes of improving the projects that lead to the creation of SoiM, extensive research has been conducted as well as feedback from the industry. As a result, users may utilize data from clouds to monitor the state of the soil without having to physically visit the plantation.

Haze Detector

Implemented during degree studies at UPM
Duration : June 2022 - July 2022

About : Project from Embedded Systems Design Course (ECC3303). This project gives the society an awareness which makes people around the area prepared if the haze gets worse and plan their next step by either moving out or wearing a mask. The system basically has two different sensors: flame sensor and PM 2.5. These sensors will be put around the forest area and inhabitant area. It detects the flame which becomes one of the sources of the haze as well as the dust particles around this area. The data is then being processed by NodeMCU ESP8266 and the output will be in Blynk application, LCD, LED, and buzzer. By accessing the result, people would be aware of the haze condition surrounding them.

Skills acquired : C++, Arduino and Basic Electrical Circuit Knowledge
